AI Native Journey

I find the problem others assumed away, then build the AI product that actually solves it.

Designing physical environments at LISAA trained me to read constraint before proposing form, a habit that transferred directly into product work. A Master's in Innovation Design Management in Germany added systems thinking, business model design, and structured validation. Enterprise delivery across healthcare, mining, logistics, and B2B commerce added the friction that turns frameworks into judgment.

The thread across all of it: I work backwards from what the system actually needs to do, not what it looks like it should do. That is why I end up cutting features before launch, reframing routing models instead of fixing UIs, and specifying AI architectures around trust constraints rather than capability lists.
